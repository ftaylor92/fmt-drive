π

π is the perimeter of a circle with a diameter of 1. π = perimeter/diameter.

High approximation or perimeter:
Perimeter(hi)= 2*r*tan(π/n)*n

Low approximation of perimeter:
Perimeter(lo)= 2*r*sin(π/n)*n

where:
n= number of sides
q= angles, or 360°/n
 r= radius, or ½ diameter


Archimedes calculated a polygon enclosing the circle (high approximation), and a polygon enclosed by the circle(low approximation), and averaged them to approximate p.

The average of Perimeter(hi) and Perimeter(lo) is p.
p= (perimeter(hi)+perimeter(lo))/2
